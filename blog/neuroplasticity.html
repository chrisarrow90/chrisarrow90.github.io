Neuroplasticity

What is Neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity is the ability of the brain to restructure or rewire itself in order to learn and adapt. Traditional thinking was brains became fully developed when you reached adulthood however there is growing evidence and recognition that this ability to restructure can occur throughout life.

The benefits of neuroplasticity is it allows us to train our brain just like how we would train our muscles to become stronger, in order to develop or keep our mental fitness and resilience. It doesn't matter if you are 9 or 90!

How does it work?

Neutroplastic change occurs at the chemical, structural and functional levels of the brain

  • Chemical Change - Occurs in the initial stages of learning something new. Primarily influences short-term memory or short-term improvement of a motor skill.
  • Structural Change - Occurs when neurons in the brain change their connections, altering your brain structure through the creation of new neurons and neural connections. This type of change requires more effort and time and involves long-term memory and long-term improvement of a motor skill.
  • Functional Change - Occurs when entire brain networks change. Brain networks become more excitable and more efficient when they are activated over and over.

How can we harness the power of Neuroplasticity?

On average, cognitive declines begins between the ages of 20 and 30. Just like an unused motor can seize up, our brain too can become degraded and the only way to prevent this from happening is with a life of continual learning and challenging the brain on a daily basis. It is truely a case of "use it or lose it".

Therefore it is key to exercise the brain and there are numerous ways to do so:

  • Video Games - There are numerous benefits associated with playing video games including motor co-ordination, visual recognition and spatial navigation, memory and reaction time, reasoning, decision making and problem solving skills as well as teaching the skills of resilience and teamwork.
  • Exercise - Exercise has been shown to improve cognitive abilities like learning and memory. It promotes blood flow and cell growth in the brain.
  • Travel - Exposure to new environments leads to synaptogenesis, which is the creation of new neural connections in the brain.
  • Sleep! - Sleep is important to the brain and helps with learning and memory retention. Naps are good.
  • Food - walnuts, blueberries, avacado. Vitamind D and magnesium are important nutrients.

Other ways to enhance neuroplasticity:

  • Learn a language or play a musical instrument
  • Non-dominant hand exercises
  • Yoga
  • Reading
  • Fasting
  • Brain training games

Growth Mindset

What is it?

A growth mindset is the belief that intelligence can be developed, this leads to a desire to learn and therefore a tendency to embrace challenges, persist in the face of setbacks, see effort as the path to mastery, learn from criticsm and find lessons and inspiration in the success of others. As a result, those with a growth mindset are able to reach even higher levels of achievement than those with a fixed mindset who instead tend to run from difficulty and give up easily. A growth mindset gives you a greater sense of free will and that your effort and persistence is a determiner of your success

Integrating into my learning journey

To integrate a growth mindset into my learning journey I will praise and reward effort rather than results or grades. I will see learning as a continual process and just because I am not capable of doing something now, does not mean that I am incapable or unintelligent, it just means that I have not yet developed the ability (embrace the power of yet). I will seek out challenges even if there is a chance of failure and instead see it as an opportunity to grow. I will not be afraid to seek help or admit when I don't know something, rather than seeing it as making me look stupid instead I will view it as a learning opportunity.

Other ways to embrace a growth mindset:

  • Embrace your imperfections
  • Process more important than the end result
  • Stop seeking approval of others - enjoy the learning process
  • Growth is more important than speed - learning takes time
  • Cultivate a sense of purpose - keep a gratitude journal
  • Talent is 99% hard work, 1% genius
  • Embrace criticism
  • "room for improvement" rather than "failure"
  • Take time to reflect
  • Take risks even if others are watching
