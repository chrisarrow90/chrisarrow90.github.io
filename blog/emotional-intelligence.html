Cultural Blog - Emotional Intelligence

1 July 2021

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence, known as EQ for short, is the ability to understand, use and manage your own emotions in positive ways to relieve stress, communicate effectively, empathise with others, overcome challenges and diffuse conflict. People with high emotional intellgence are able to recognise their own emotions and those of others and use emotional information to guide their thinking and behaviour, adapting it to their environments.

Emotional intelligence helps you build stronger relationships, succeed at school and work and achieve your career and personal goals. It can also help you connect with your feelings, turn intention into action and make informed decisions about what matters most to you.

What are the differences between IQ and EQ?

IQ refers to your intellectual ability or mental capability. In general this is:

  • Ability to reason
  • Plan and strategise
  • Use logic to solve problems
  • Think abstractly
  • Comprehend complex ideas

Emotional intelligence on the other hand is the ability to identify, assess and control the emotions of oneself, of others and of groups. The main attributes of Emotional intelligence are:

  • Self-management - ability to control impulsive feelings and beaviours, manage your emotions in healthy ways, take initiative and follow through on commitments.
  • Self-awareness - ability to recognise your own emotions and how they affect your thoughts and behaviours. Knowledge of your strengths and weaknesses and having self-confidence
  • Social awareness - can understand the emotions, needs and concerns of other people, pick up on emotional cues, feel comfortable socially and recognise the power dynamics in a group or organisation. Have empathy.
  • Relationship management - know how to develop and maintain relationships, communiate clearly, inspire and influence others, work well in a team and manage conflict

Why is Emotional Intelligence important?

Historically, scores on IQ tests have been linked to better academic performance, higher salaries and better job performances however EQ has been linked to job success as well as more satisfying relationships. Studies have also shown that people with higher EQ have greater mental health, job performance and leadersip skills.

Employers are placing greater emphasis on Emotional intelligence training and screening as part of the hiring process. It is valued by employers who are looking for candidates who can complete work and gett along with people in progressively collaborative workplaces.

It's importance in the workplace includes the areas of:

  • People Development - enables people to grow, develop and master their strengths
  • Influencing - useful for persuasion and negotation skills
  • communication - ability to communicate clear and effectively
  • Leadership - inspiring and guiding groups of people
  • Conflict Resolution - negotiating and resolving disagreements
  • Relationships - nurturing bonds for business success
  • Collaboration and Co-operation - working wtih coworkers and business partners towards shared goals
  • Team Capabilities - group synergy in pursuing collective goals
