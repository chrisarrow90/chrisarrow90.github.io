Core Reflection

20 July 2021

Reflection

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

I discovered more about my personality type and the areas I need to be concious about particularly when working in group environments such as to avoid coming across as or being reserved. Also I need to be careful with my communication style and be concious of others' emotions/feelings (as Virtuosos can tend to have a blunt way of communicating since they deal with logic and facts).

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

Empathy is important for developing strong relationships and group cohesion. It allows us to connect with and work at a deeper level with our customers and colleagues. By understanding and respecting their needs and emotions, it will lead to a more happier and productive workplace and produce better results. Empathy also enables us to better deal with conflicts and disagreements.

Self awareness allows us to reflect on how we learn best and therefore we can adjust our learning styles and environment in a way that suits us best. It also allows us to identify our areas of weakness, or when we are felling frustrated and therefore we will be able to take steps to rectify these situations before they become a problem.

What has surprised you the most about core learning?

Coming from a background as a Pilot, alot of these concepts are familiar as I have spent a lot of time learning about Human Factors and Performance. It is interesting to note many of the soft-skills required to be a pilot equally apply to the Web Development and Programming industry.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Self-reflection has been the most challenging aspect as I often find it hard to write about myself or my thoughts. Also writing blogs/creative writing is not my forte.

Why do you think we are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootccamp course?

Because soft skills are cruicial to being a well rounded programmer and employers are placing increasing value on soft skills, just as much as, if not more than technical skills. They are probably also the hardest to develop and requires a lot of self reflection which we typically do not do much of.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead?

No. I think one of the greatest benefit from this program comes from the huge focus on core learning and soft skills. If I just wanted to learn programming there would be plenty of free online resources to do so. But the greatest value in this course will come from the soft skills development. The core learning during foundations has been very insightful and useful.
